How it started

A two-day workshop that changed everything

OrbitNext was not a directive from the top. It was built in a room, by the people who run this organisation, through two days of honest conversation about where InApp needed to go next.

Guided by Sankar Krishnan and Divya Krishnan, our leaders came together to map strategy, confront bottlenecks, and set bold ambitions. Inspiration became action. Bold ideas became a shared roadmap.

“Business growth is a sequence of S-curves. You do something, see growth, then plateau, and then you must reinvent to reach the Next orbit.”
Dr. Anand Deshpande, Founder & Chairman, Persistent Systems — whose words ignited the turning point conversation

That session became the turning point. The workshop charted OrbitNext — a bold roadmap to deliver greater value, greater confidence, and greater outcomes for every client we serve.

InApp 25th Anniversary Celebration — August 2025
The full ambition

12 Major Moves.
One shared direction.

The Management Team workshop defined 12 areas where InApp needed to move boldly. These became the blueprint for everything that followed.

01
Nearshore & Onshore Presence
Establish delivery and support teams in nearshore and onshore locations to strengthen client confidence and tap into local markets.
02
Strengthening Marketing
Significantly increase investments in marketing to boost visibility, lead generation, and positioning.
03
Augmenting Tech Teams
Hire 5–10 senior technology leaders to elevate solution design, tech governance, and innovation.
04
AI Enabled Organisation
Ensure every team has AI literacy and is cloud-competent; aim for full org readiness.
05
Innovation Engine
Institutionalize a dedicated innovation charter; 25% of work to be innovation-led.
06
Delivery Capabilities
Build predictable, scalable delivery models with better governance, tools, and execution depth.
07
Productize
Identify and evolve at least 5 reusable modules including MLOps or functional accelerators.
08
Strengthen Account Management
Build a world-class account management function capable of handling large enterprise clients.
09
Customer First
Reorient the org to track and deliver customer outcomes, not just delivery completion.
10
People Initiatives
Launch 2–3 people-centric initiatives to energize and engage at least 350 employees.
11
Lateral Investments in Talent
Expand the current team with 30–50 specialized lateral hires across key functions.
12
L&D Function
Build a strong Learning & Development function to support leadership, tech, and delivery capabilities.
